## The Evolution of UPI: From 2016 to 10 Billion Daily Transactions

UPI was officially launched on **April 11, 2016**, by the **National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI)** in collaboration with 21 banks. Built on the **IMPS infrastructure**, it allowed **instantaneous bank-to-bank transfers**.

### Launch of UPI by NPCI and Its Early Adoption

- Introduced **Virtual Payment Addresses (VPA)** to simplify transactions without account numbers or IFSC codes.  
- Demonetization in late 2016 accelerated adoption as citizens sought cashless alternatives.  
- Initial growth: **92,000 transactions in August 2016**, rapidly expanding afterward.  
- Banks partnered with NPCI to integrate UPI into existing apps, improving accessibility.

- Aadhaar enabled **secure biometric authentication**.

## Blockchain Integration in UPI: The 2025 Breakthrough

In 2025, NPCI integrated **distributed ledger technology (DLT)** to improve UPI's scalability, security, and automation.

### DLT-Based Settlement Layer for Interbank Transactions

- **Vajra**: Blockchain-based settlement system for UPI.  
- Processes API requests via bank nodes and records validated data on-chain.  
- Benefits: faster settlements, minimal reconciliation, improved resilience, reduced operational risk.

### Smart Contract Automation for Recurring Payments

- **MandatMatrix**: Blockchain-powered UPI Autopay platform (Hyperledger Fabric).  
- Processes **1,000+ transactions/sec**, handled 10M transactions in 4 hours during initial deployment.  
- Real-time updates of mandate status, trustless and immutable ledger.

## Technical Infrastructure Behind the Scale

### Zero-Downtime Architecture Using Distributed Nodes

- Oracle Globally Distributed Database deployed across regions.  
- Active-active deployment ensures payments remain online even if one region fails.  
- Key technologies: Cassandra, MariaDB, PostgreSQL, KeyDB.

### API Standardization and Open Banking Protocols

- Centralized API switch simplifies integration for banks and third-party providers (Google Pay, PhonePe).  
- Users transact using **mobile numbers or QR codes** without full account info.  
- Standardized protocols maintain system integrity amid growth.

### Load Balancing and Real-Time Reconciliation Systems

- NPCI switch intelligently routes payment requests to optimal servers.  
- Real-time reconciliation reduces errors by 30% and reconciliation time by 50%.
